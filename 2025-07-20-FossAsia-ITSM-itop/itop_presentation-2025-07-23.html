What is ITSM?

Definition: IT Service Management (ITSM)

Aligning IT services with business needs

Improving service delivery & customer satisfaction

Managing incidents, changes, and assets efficiently

Why ITSM matters in modern IT operations

Without ITSM: Companies face chaotic incident resolution and poor alignment with business goals

ITSM Frameworks & Standards

ITIL

Information Technology Infrastructure Library - Most widely adopted

FitSM

Lightweight ITSM framework - Great for smaller organizations

ISO/IEC 20000

International ITSM Standard - For compliance-heavy industries

COBIT

Governance & Risk Management framework

Key Insight: Your tool should support the framework you follow

Question: ServiceNow dominates the enterprise market, but is it always the best fit?

Why Choose Open Source?

Evolution of ITSM Tools:

Manual/Paper → Homegrown Solutions → Open Source → SaaS

Advantages of Open Source:

  • Reuse It – Leverage existing solutions
  • Own It – No vendor lock-in, full control
  • License Freedom – Avoid recurring costs
  • Community-Driven Innovation

When Paid/SaaS Makes Sense: Companies wanting hands-off maintenance

Bottom Line: The best choice depends on your budget, team size, and IT maturity

How to Select the Right ITSM Tool

Key Considerations:

Business needs & scalability

Integration capabilities

Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)

Community & support availability

Compliance with ITIL/FitSM

Remember: Don't just pick the 'best' tool—pick the one that fits your workflow
